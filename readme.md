## ðŸ§  2. Core Architecture: Nodes, Themes, and Systems Thinking

The framework is built on a library of **Nodes** (specific actions) categorized by **Themes** (cognitive stances) and rigorously analyzed by their impact on the overall system.

### A. The Four Themes (Cognitive Stances)

| Theme | Stance | Key Question | Example Nodes |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Blueprint** | **Architectural** | What is the ideal structure? | `Define a Problem Statement` (`N02`), `Create a Work Breakdown Structure` (`N24`) |
| **Workshop** | **Enactive** | How do we build and learn by doing? | `Build a Prototype` (`N28`), `Execute an Experiment` (`N26`) |
| **Critique** | **Evaluative** | What does the feedback indicate, and how do we fix it? | `Analyze Quantitative Data` (`N33`), `Conduct QA Testing` (`N35`) |
| **Council** | **Relational** | Whose voices must be heard, and what are our ethical duties? | `Pull the 'Ethical Red Flag'` (`N01`), `Hold a Council` (`N65`) |

### B. Systems Thinking Analysis (The "Why")

Every node is analyzed by its effect on a dynamic system, providing a deep understanding of its leverage:

*   **Stock:** What resource does this node fill or deplete? (e.g., `N01` protects the stock of **'Trust'**).
*   **Inflow/Outflow:** How does this node regulate the flow of resources (time, money, ideas)? (e.g., `N09` **reduces the outflow of 'Team Cohesion'**).
*   **Valve:** What is the policy or rule that controls the flow? (e.g., `N52` is the **Valve on the 'Inflow of Resources'**).
*   **Feedback Loop:** Does this node initiate a **Reinforcing (R)** loop (growth) or a **Balancing (B)** loop (stability)? (e.g., `N72` initiates a **Balancing Loop for 'System Reliability'**).
